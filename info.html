Understanding Income Tax Regimes (2024-25)

India offers two tax regimes: the Old Tax Regime and the New Tax Regime.

Old Tax Regime

The Old Tax Regime allows taxpayers to claim deductions and exemptions to reduce their taxable income.

  • Suitable for salaried individuals: People with fixed salaries can plan their tax savings by utilizing deductions and exemptions.
  • Reduces taxable income significantly: If you claim maximum deductions, your tax liability can be much lower compared to the New Tax Regime.

  • Higher tax rates: The tax rates in this regime are higher than those in the New Tax Regime.

New Tax Regime

The New Tax Regime offers lower tax rates but removes most deductions and exemptions.

  • Not beneficial for high earners: If you have a high income and can claim many deductions, you may end up paying more tax in this regime.
  • No incentives for savings and investments: Unlike the Old Regime, this does not encourage tax-saving investments like PPF, NPS, and life insurance.
  • Less suitable for salaried individuals with exemptions: If you receive HRA, LTA, and other allowances, you lose those benefits.
